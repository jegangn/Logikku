---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- A **white dot** between two cells means they are consecutive, differing by one.
- A **black dot** means one cell is exactly double the other.

---
title: Quick example
---
A black dot links a pair that must be `1` and `2`, `2` and `4`, `3` and `6`, or `4` and `8`. With no dot between cells, they are neither consecutive nor in a one-to-two ratio.
