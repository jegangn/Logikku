---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- A **circled cell** equals the sum of the digits along its arrow.
- Add up every cell the arrow passes through to match the circle.

---
title: Quick example
---
If an arrow covers two cells and the circle holds `7`, those two cells must add to 7, such as `3` and `4`. A long arrow forces small digits, since their total still has to fit in one circle.
