---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- An **arrow outside the grid** gives a total for the diagonal it points along.
- Add the digits on that diagonal to match the printed sum.

---
title: Quick example
---
If an outside arrow marks a two-cell diagonal with sum 4, those cells must be `1` and `3`. Short diagonals near the corners give the tightest, most useful totals.
