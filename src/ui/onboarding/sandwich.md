---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- A number **outside a row or column** is the sum of the digits trapped between the `1` and the `9`.
- The `1` and `9` themselves are not counted.

---
title: Quick example
---
A clue of `0` means the `1` and `9` sit right next to each other, with nothing between them. A large clue means many digits are sandwiched, which limits where the `1` and `9` can go.
