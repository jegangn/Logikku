---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- A grey **path** marks a line of cells that reads the same both ways.
- The first and last cells match, the second and second-to-last match, and so on.

---
title: Quick example
---
On a five-cell palindrome, the ends must be equal and the cells just inside them must be equal too. If one end is fixed as `4`, the matching end is also `4`, copying values across the line.
