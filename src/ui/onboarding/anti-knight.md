---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- No two cells a **chess knight's move** apart may share the same digit.
- A knight's move is two cells one way and one cell across.

---
title: Quick example
---
If a cell holds `5`, then every cell a knight's move away cannot be `5`. This rules out digits in surprising spots across the grid, far from the usual row and column lines.
