---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- An **arrow** between two cells points from the larger digit to the smaller.
- The cell at the wide open end is greater than the cell at the point.

---
title: Quick example
---
If an arrow points away from a cell holding `2`, the neighbour at the point must be `1`. Chains of arrows force a steady rise or fall that you can follow cell by cell.
