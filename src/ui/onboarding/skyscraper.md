---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- Each digit is a **building** of that height, and taller buildings hide shorter ones behind them.
- A number outside the grid counts how many buildings you can see from that side.

---
title: Quick example
---
A clue of `1` means the nearest building is the tallest, so the first cell is `9`. A clue of `9` means every building is visible, so that line climbs `1, 2, 3` and onward in order.
