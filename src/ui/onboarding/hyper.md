---
title: How it works
---
- The usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- Four extra shaded 3×3 **windows** sit inside the grid.
- Each window must also hold 1–9 with no repeats.

---
title: Quick example
---
If a shaded window already shows the digits `1` through `8`, its remaining cell must be `9`, even if the row and column have not yet forced it. The windows often crack the puzzle open earlier.
