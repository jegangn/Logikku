---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- Cells marked with a **grey square** must hold an even digit: 2, 4, 6, or 8.
- Cells marked with a **circle** must hold an odd digit: 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.

---
title: Quick example
---
A square cell can only be `2`, `4`, `6`, or `8`, so half the digits are gone before you start. Use those parity marks to thin out the choices in tight rows and boxes.
