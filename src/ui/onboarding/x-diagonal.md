---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- The two main **diagonals**, corner to corner, must also hold 1–9.
- A digit can appear only once along each diagonal.

---
title: Quick example
---
If a diagonal already contains `1` through `8`, its last empty cell must be `9`. The diagonals give you extra cells to reason about beyond the standard rows and columns.
