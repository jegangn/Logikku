---
title: How it works
---
- All the usual rules apply: rows, columns, and boxes each hold 1–9.
- No two cells a **chess king's move** apart may share the same digit.
- That means no touching cells, including diagonals, can match.

---
title: Quick example
---
If a cell holds `3`, none of its eight surrounding neighbours may be `3` either. This forbids matching digits from sitting diagonally next to each other, which classic rules allow.
